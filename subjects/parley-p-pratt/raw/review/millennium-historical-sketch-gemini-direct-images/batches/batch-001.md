HISTORICAL SKETCH

FROM THE

CREATION TO THE PRESENT DAY.

In Three Parts.

PART FIRST.

When earth's foundation first was laid,<br />
The heavens in order stood;<br />
And all the works God's hand had made,<br />
His word pronounced good.

But soon the happy scene was changed,<br />
For man to whom 'twas given,<br />
To choose the way of life or death,<br />
Trangressed the law of heaven.

[[page 32]]

And thus the evil seed was sown,<br />
And death through all their race;<br />
In which creation long has groaned;<br />
In pain to be released.

'T was then the scene of love began<br />
To be revealed on earth;<br />
By angels borne from heaven to man<br />
The gospel's heavenly birth.

The God of heaven shall send his son,<br />
For man to bleed and die;<br />
And rise again that man may rise,<br />
And reign with him on high.

Repentance and baptism then<br />
By angels were revealed,<br />
The holy ghost descending down,<br />
The heirs of glory seal'd.

Thus men began to exercise<br />
Their faith in Jesus' word,<br />
With joy to embrace the gospel plan,<br />
And call upon the Lord.

But many then would not believe,<br />
But soon forgot the Lord;<br />
Soon Enoch rose with mighty power,<br />
Being call'd to preach the word.

He preach'd repentance and baptized,<br />
Through all the happy land,<br />
The people who in Zion dwelt,<br />
Were of one heart and mind.

At length the city was not found,<br />
For God received it up;<br />
The residue were left to drown,<br />
And in the prison shut.

But Noah the Eighth was saved by faith,<br />
When warn'd an ark to build,<br />
And seven of his family,<br />
From whom the earth was fill'd.

PART SECOND.

Again the nations left the Lord<br />
To worship stocks and stones,<br />
Forgot the wonders of the flood,<br />
And sunk in darkness down;

And then again was God reveal'd<br />
To Abram, his friend,<br />
Called him to leave his house and home,<br />
To view a chosen land.

To thee and to thy seed, he said,<br />
I give this blessed land,<br />
Though like the stars for multitude,<br />
And numerous as the sand.

But Abraham died a stranger in<br />
The land 'twas to him given,<br />
Nor owned a place to set his foot,<br />
On it beneath the heaven.

His seed possessed it for a while,<br />
Became a sinful host;<br />
And then ten tribes were led away,<br />
And to our knowledge lost.

From time to time were led away,<br />
Of Israel's chosen seed,<br />
Dispersed o'er islands of the sea,<br />
As all the prophets read.

And thus the ages rolled away,<br />
The appointed time drew near,<br />
As all the prophets had declared<br />
That Christ must soon appear.

John, like a bright and morning star<br />
Rose to prepare his way,<br />
Proclaimed repentance, and baptized<br />
Whoever would obey.

The son of God at length appeared,<br />
And was baptized by John,<br />
The Father sent the spirit down<br />
And owned him for his son.

He to his own the gospel preached,<br />
His own received him not,<br />
Despised all his mighty works,<br />
And counted him as naught.

At length their Lord they crucified,<br />
While nature stood amazed,<br />
The solid rocks in sunder rent,<br />
While Jew and Gentile gazed.

But soon the third bright morn appeared,<br />
When, rising from the dead,<br />
To his disciples he appeared<br />
And thus unto them said:

PART THIRD.

Go ye, and preach in all the world,<br />
Baptizing in my name,<br />
He that believes and is baptized<br />
Salvation shall obtain.

Then rising from Mount Olivet<br />
Unto his Father's throne,<br />
On high to reign until he claims<br />
The kingdoms for his own.
