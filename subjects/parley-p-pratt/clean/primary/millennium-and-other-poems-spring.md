# Spring

WRITTEN IN PRISON, APRIL, 1839.

See nature bursting into life and bloom:<br>
Joyous, it rises from its wintry tomb,<br>
Decked in pure robes of purple, white, or green:<br>
Perfumed with incense sweet—O lovely scene!<br>
Melodious sounds, with music soft and sweet,<br>
Thrill through the air—thy joyous presence greet.<br>
Behold, O Mary! and remember too,<br>
There is a spring to bloom for me and you;—<br>
We, like the spring, shall burst the sullen gloom.<br>
All clothed in white—eternally to bloom.<br>
We too, will join the choir his praise to sing,<br>
And hail the welcome of Eternal Spring.
