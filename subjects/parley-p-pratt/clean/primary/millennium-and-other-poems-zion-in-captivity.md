# Zion in Captivity

A LAMENTATION.

WRITTEN IN PRISON.

Torn from our friends and captive led,<br>
’Mid armed legions bound in chains,<br>
That peace for which our fathers bled<br>
Is gone, and dire confusion reigns.

Zion, our peaceful happy home,<br>
Where oft we joined in praise and prayer,<br>
A desolation has become,<br>
And grief and sorrow linger there.

Her virgins sigh, her widows mourn,<br>
Her children for their parents weep;<br>
In chains her priests and prophets groan,<br>
While some in deaths cold arms do sleep.

Exultingly her savage foes<br>
Now ravage, steal and plunder, where<br>
A virgin’s, tears, a widow’s woes,<br>
Became their song of triumph there.

How long, O Lord, wilt thou forsake<br>
The saints who tremble at thy word?<br>
Awake, O arm of God, awake—<br>
And teach the nations thou art God.

Descend with all thy holy throng,<br>
The year of thy redeem’d bring near;<br>
Haste—haste the day of vengeance on—<br>
Bid Zion’s children dry their tears.

Deliver, Lord, thy captive saints,<br>
And comfort those who long have mourn’d;<br>
Bid Zion cease her dire complaints,<br>
And all creation cease to groan.
