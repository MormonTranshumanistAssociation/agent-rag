# Reflections on a Summer Evening

Another day has fled and gone,<br>
The sun declines in western skies,<br>
The birds retired, have ceased their song,<br>
Let ours in pure devotion rise.

The moon her splendid course resumes,<br>
She sheds her light o’er land and sea,<br>
The gentle dews in soft perfumes<br>
Fall sweetly o’er each herb and tree.

While here in meditation sweet,<br>
Those happy hours I call to mind,<br>
When with the saints I oft have met,<br>
Our hearts in pure devotion joined.

Those friends afar I call to mind,<br>
When shall we meet again below;<br>
Their hearts affectionate and kind,<br>
How did they soothe my grief and woe.

As flow’rets in their brightest bloom,<br>
Are withered by the chilling blast,<br>
So man’s fond hopes are like a dream,<br>
His days how fleet, how swift they pass.

But cease this melancholy moan,<br>
Nor sigh for those who will not come,<br>
For Israel surely will return<br>
To Zion and Jerusalem.

There is a source of pure delight<br>
For ever shall support my heart:<br>
For Zion’s land’s revealed to sight,<br>
Where saints will meet no more to part.
