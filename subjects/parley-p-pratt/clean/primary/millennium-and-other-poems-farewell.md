# Farewell

Farewell, my kind and faithful friend—<br>
The partner of my early youth,<br>
While from my home my steps I bend,<br>
To warn mankind and teach the truth.

How oft in silent evening mild,<br>
I to some lonely place retire—<br>
Thy love and kindness call to mind,<br>
Then lift a voice in humble prayer.

O Lord, extend thine arms of love,<br>
Around the partner of my heart,<br>
For thou hast spoken from above,<br>
And called me with my all to part.

Preserve her soul in perfect peace,<br>
From sickness, sorrow and distress,<br>
Until our pilgrimage shall cease.<br>
And we on Zion’s hill shall rest.

How gladly would my soul retire<br>
With thee, to spend a peaceful life,<br>
In some sequestered humble vale,<br>
Far from the scenes of noise and strife.

Where men should grieve our souls no more,<br>
Nor rage of sin disturb our peace;<br>
Our troubles, toils and sufferings o’er,<br>
Their lies and persecutions cease.

## Part Second

But lo! the harvest wide extends—<br>
The fields are white o’er all the plain—<br>
The tares in bundles must be bound,<br>
While we with care secure the grain.

Shall we repine when Jesus calls,<br>
Or count the sacrifice too great,<br>
To spend our lives as pilgrims here,<br>
Or loose them for the gospel’s sake?

When Jesus Christ has done the same,<br>
Without a place to lay his head,<br>
A pilgrim on the earth he came,<br>
Until for us his blood was shed.

Shall we behold the nations doomed<br>
To sword and famine, blood and fire,<br>
Yet not the least exertion make,<br>
But from the scene in peace retire?

No; while his love for me extends,<br>
The pattern makes my duty plain—<br>
I’ll sound to earth’s remotest ends,<br>
His gospel to the sons of men!

Farewell, my kind and faithful friend,<br>
Until we meet on earth again—<br>
For soon our pilgrimage shall end,<br>
And the Messiah come to reign.
